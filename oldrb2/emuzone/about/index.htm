History.

It all began back in May 1997 when Johnny 'Sonikku' Wallbank had an idea, he wanted to make a Sonic fan-game. At this time, Sonic fan-games were relatively rare around the Internet, in comparison to today, where you can download hundreds of fan games.

So, loading up Klik & Play, Sonikku sat down at work, and for the next 9 months, worked on 'Sonic Robo Blast'. The largest Sonic game to date ever made in Klik & Play, with two hours worth of levels to fight your way through.

After releasing the final game in February 1998, Sonikku took a month off and started planning work on Sonic Robo Blast 2 (SRB2). In May 1998, Sonic Team Junior was formed, with the first two members, Sonikku and David Bulmer working on two projects: SRB2 and Sonic Quest (the latter of which is now re-named to 'Sonic: Emerald Quest RPG'.

Soon, people were sending designs and graphics to join - giving us the team today. Sonic Robo Blast 2 underwent several engine changes, from an enhanced SRB1 engine, to a new 'Games Factory' engine, to C and finally to 3D.

Future Projects.

We're not giving up after SRB2 - we've got an RPG in line, which is already in planning stages. We hope to release single and multi-player addons for Sonic Robo Blast 2, with new levels, patches, 'skins' and other goodies to keep SRB2 going long after it's released.

Beyond that... well... who knows?
