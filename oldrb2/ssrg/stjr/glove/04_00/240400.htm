24th April 2000 -

The stuff people say…

 

Welcome to the first of the Off-Topic White Glove Diaries! This week, I’ll be getting p/oed about this Violence in Video Games tripe.

 

This obviously doesn’t pertain at all to SRB2, and perhaps only a little to S:EQ-RPG, so if you’re looking for stuff on either of these, you won’t find ‘em here.

 

“Violence in video-games is wrong. It will convert a normal happy 8-year-old into a gun wielding maniac.” – Oops. It will? Well, 7 or so years after I played DooM I as an 8-year-old and I’m still not running around outside with a shotgun shooting hell-spawn or real people. It’s also not happened with DooM II, Quake, Quake II, Unreal Tournament and Half-Life. Am I missing something here?

 

OK, so there are some people who will get wound up one day and start shooting everyone they see and then claim it’s all Unreal Tournament’s fault as they thought they were in a Deathmatch arena. The number of people who will do this in about a decade will be... what…? 15? 30? How many people will die of smoking over a decade? Several million. If you were the government, which one would you stamp out?

 

If anything, shoot-em-ups are a great way to relieve stress. If I have a bad day at school, I launch up any of the above with bots and have a quick 15-minute Deathmatch game. In that time I have relieved all my stress on a bunch of unrealistic polygons. Or I make SRB2 levels. Neither of which make me go insane. (Except when I have something go badly wrong in designing SRB2 levels…)

 

If the government really wants to go OTT (Over the top) about all this, then they could try actually enforcing the law where games must be banned. I picked up Command and Conquer: Red Alert a few years ago and didn’t know that ELSPA had rated it an ‘18+ only’ until I opened the box when I got home (I must have been 12 or 13 then). Nor did it even feel like an 18+ game, and it still baffles me today how it got ‘18+’.

 

Thankfully, they’re not banned, it’s just a recommendation, I’m not entirely sure on the law, but I think if the BBFC slap a ‘12’, ’15’ or ‘18’ sticker on the game, it’s not a recommendation, it’s required you are above that age to play it. Usually, only reserved for ultra-violent ‘realistic’ games with a sprinkling of sexual scenes. Oh, or games with head-butting.

 

Time for a humorous diversion! In England, I recently found out that if I film or game contains a single head-butt, then it is immediately declared ‘extremely violent’ and it is ‘realistic’. In the same way, of course, that if a guy bursting into a room and shooting hundreds of agents isn’t considered the above. Nope. Of course not.

 

Then I found out that, woe betide the BBFC – Mulan (the DVD version) had remained uncut and HAD A HEADBUTT IN IT! MY GOD! THE COUNTRY WILL BE FILLED WITH LUNATIC 5-YEAR OLDS!!! Or alternatively, the BBFC are going postal for no reason whatsoever. I still haven’t found the BBC news reading, “Today, a 5-year old was found dead in his home, after another boy of the same age repeatedly head-butted him.”

 

My point? The government should set it’s priorities right, and not use video-games as a something to blame everything on.

 

Meanwhile, I’m off to head-butt millions after I saw that head-butt on Mulan, which has possessed my brain. Must… head-butt… people… must… head-butt…
