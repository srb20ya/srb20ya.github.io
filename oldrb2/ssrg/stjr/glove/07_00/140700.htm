14 July 2000 -

Video-game piracy.

 

I was considering doing something on this, but Sega’s ‘Pirate Hotline’ has made me sit up and do say something. Why? It’s ridiculous.

 

A.J Freda and I had a brief discussion on this, and I’ll mention some of the points we brought up here. Why do top-Sony / Sega reps claim that piracy is ‘bad, evil!’ when they own expensive cars and the like? It’s good business, that’s why.

 

Think about it – Sony decided to bust a pirate company a while back, with (according to Digitiser) Tommy Lee Jones heading the operation. Why Tommy Lee Jones? People will then go and think: “Oh wow! Tommy Lee Jones is getting involved with the PlayStation! It must be good! Let’s go and buy one!” PC games were very popular back in the days when you get a whole game by just copying three floppies. Not bad.

 

Let’s compare sales. The PlayStation and PlayStation II are still selling by the truckload in Japan. The Dreamcast is not. What’s the link? Think how easy it is to pirate the PlayStation. The PlayStation II mod chip has already been cracked. The Dreamcast has only just had the mod chip come out. Piracy is good business. Word gets around about how ‘cheap’ games are and the main system sells like hotcakes. Friends can tell each other in advance if xyz game is good or worth buying. (Who’ll then get the legitimate copy, assuming they don’t have a mod chip).

 

The other advantage of pirated games is the fact they get around import and ‘super’ import locks. By ‘super’ import locks, I mean that some games will detect the presence of a mod-chip and refuse to work. Pirates can get around this. Square Europe is refusing to release Chrono Cross over here. Thanks a bunch. Now I don’t have much choice left, do I?

 

So – what’s my beef over the new ‘Sega Piracy Hotline’? Sony handled the piracy issue by looking big – Tommy Lee Jones involved in a major sting operation sounds a little more crowd-pleasing than Sega’s ‘Hey! Piracy is evil! We must stop it! Phone us! …Please phone us! Please? Please…?’ – Sega makes this sound like piracy is a huge problem.

 

That’s my take on the situation, anyway.
