1st June 2000 -

The dark side of online gaming…

 

After a long hiatus, I’m back with a load of White Glove Diary entries, and they’re not going to come much quicker with end of year exams around the corner… in any case – this time I’ll be talking about online gaming again, only this time with a slightly less positive attitude.

 

Bundled with my new PC was a copy of Worms Armageddon. “Hurray!” I said. I had a few single player games and after completing about three-quarters of the game, I thought it would have been a good idea to try battling some players all over the world. A quarter of an hour later and I’m into my first game – and first impressions are good, there isn’t any lag to worry about while moving your worms around (there is lag between turns, which I don’t mind waiting for that.) and I had quite a lot of fun. However, inevitably, it wasn’t long before things got bad. The Idiot patrol came by.

 

Firstly, one of the said idiots popped onto the game channels and started flooding it. It wasn’t long before he was kicked off however, which provided humour for most of us, but it was a slight sign of things going wrong.

 

Eventually, I came across some bad losers. These people will quickly and conveniently ‘accidentally’ disconnect if you’re about to kill their last worm. Or – worse still, they’ll nuke you off, meaning you’ll be the one to disconnect, and you’ll lose the game for doing so. (And if you do ‘disconnect’ several times, then you’ll be unable to play the game online again) Sure guys – that’s really skilful.

 

Thankfully, the game has a profanity filter to avoid all the psychological warfare they throw against you. In one match, the chat messages read mostly something along the lines of this:

 

“U ******* GAY-*** ****!!!!!!”

“U R A **** AND A WHORE!”

“PIECE OF **** FAG *** ******!”

“<Insert random string of swearwords, homophobic rants etc.>”

 

I ignored him and kept playing on, and eventually, he resorted to flooding me off, which actually seemed to slow him down more, as he did it between his turn. Riiigghhhttt.

 

I have no qualms with the Internet being more accessible to people, but if it means that we’re going to see more people who think that in order to win any game of Worms, they must nuke people off, then perhaps this means that online gaming will need tighter restrictions for those who want to play the game and have fun…
